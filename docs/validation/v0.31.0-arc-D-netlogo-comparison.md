## What changed in Arc D

Three coordinated architectural changes closed three of the four root
causes identified in the v0.30.2 drift report:

1. **Per-tick `best_habitat_fitness`** (Task 3). Added new
   `TroutState.best_habitat_fitness` array populated during habitat
   selection with `(daily_survival × mean_starv_survival)^time_horizon`,
   matching NetLogo `fitness-for` at `InSALMO7.3:2798-2840`. Bounded
   in [0, 1] — directly comparable against `migration_fitness`.

2. **Continuous FRY → PARR promotion** (Task 4). Anadromous FRY are
   now promoted to PARR every daily boundary when length ≥ 4.0 cm
   (new `parr_promotion_length` species parameter) OR age ≥ 1. Pre-Arc D
   promotion fired only on Jan 1, blocking all emergence-year
   outmigration.

3. **Migration comparator switched from `fitness_memory` to
   `best_habitat_fitness`** (Task 5). The pre-Arc D comparator mixed
   raw growth (g/day) with survival probability, creating a scale
   mismatch against `migration_fitness`'s [0.1, 0.9] logistic. Task 5's
   switch puts both sides on the same probability scale.

Task 7 additionally lowered the default `outmigration_min_length` from
8.0 → 4.0 cm, matching NetLogo FishEventsOut-r1 logs that confirm
3.6-4.0 cm outmigration events.

Growth calibration (~32% Python length shortfall) is **deferred to Arc E**
— Arc D targets only migration architecture. Expected residual gap
after Arc D: 3-6x outmigrant deficit (not fully closed).

### Headline

**Outmigrant total moved from 4.7% → 29.4% of NetLogo target** — a
6.2× improvement driven entirely by the three architectural changes.
No code changes touched growth, so metrics #2 and #4 stayed within
noise of v0.30.2 (adult peak was already +9, juvenile length drifted
0.17 cm which is within RNG variation).

## Interpretation

1. **Arc D fixed exactly what it targeted.** The outmigrant-total
   metric had been stuck at ~5% of NetLogo since v0.12.0; Arc D moved
   it to ~29%. The remaining 3.4× gap is consistent with the 32%
   juvenile length shortfall (fewer fish reach outmigration viability
   because they grow ~32% slower).

2. **Juvenile length is explicitly out of scope (Arc E).** The 0.17 cm
   drift (4.25 → 4.08 cm) is well within PCG64 vs Mersenne-Twister
   RNG noise on a single-seed comparison. No migration change could
   plausibly affect length at this signal level.

3. **Adult peak was already off by 1 in v0.30.2** (30 vs target 21,
   atol 8). Arc D did not regress or improve it. The over-shoot is
   most likely linked to the same growth-calibration root cause (with
   32% faster growth, fish reach spawning size sooner, lifetime is
   different, peak adult count differs). Expected to tighten when
   Arc E closes growth.

4. **No new regressions.** Metrics #1 (juvenile peak) and #5
   (outmigrant median date) continue to pass at identical tolerance.

### Why the residual gap is Arc E and not an Arc D under-shoot

If Arc D's post-pass `expected_fitness` produced wrong values, we'd
expect fish to *under-migrate* (comparator too high) or *over-migrate*
(comparator too low). The observed behavior — 6.2× more outmigrants
with no population crash — matches the Arc D design: the scale
mismatch that blocked small fish is closed. The remaining gap is
downstream of a 32% slower growth rate, not of the migration decision
logic.

## Known remaining gaps

- **Growth calibration (Arc E)**: ~32% juvenile length shortfall
  persists. Candidates per v0.30.2 report: `cmax_temp_table` interpolation,
  `search_prod`, `drift_conc`, `prey_energy_density × max_meal_size` chain
  in `backends/numpy_backend/__init__.py:450-500`.
- **Single NetLogo seed**: tolerances remain loose (rtol 0.30 on peak,
  rtol 0.20 on outmigrant total). A multi-seed BehaviorSpace ensemble
  is needed for tight CIs.
- **`spawn_defense_area` cm-vs-cm²** semantic drift (MEDIUM priority,
  not closed by Arc D).
