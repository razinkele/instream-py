# v0.30.2 Python ↔ NetLogo InSALMO Run-Level Parity Report

**Date:** 2026-04-19
**inSTREAM-py version:** 0.30.2
**NetLogo reference:** InSALMO 7.3 (NetLogo 6.4.0) + InSALMO 7.4 (NetLogo 7.0.3), both byte-identical on example_a metrics
**Reference seed:** 98 (single seed only — no ensemble CI yet)
**Test fixture:** `example_a` (Chinook spring+fall, single-reach, 2011-04-01 → 2013-09-30)
**Test location:** `tests/test_run_level_parity.py::TestExampleARunVsNetLogo`

## Motivation

Until this report, NetLogo parity coverage for inSTREAM-py was
*function-level only*: 17 deterministic-function tests in
`tests/test_validation.py` (CMax, CStepMax, growth, survival-5-sources,
spawn suitability, fitness). No whole-model run-level comparison had
ever been executed against the cached BehaviorSpace tables sitting in
`netlogo-models/`, despite both codebases covering the same 2.5-year
example_a span.

The Sprint-4 deliverable in `docs/NETLOGO_PARITY_ROADMAP.md` called for
"behavioral checks beyond unit tests". Arc C of today's v0.30.2 plan
closes it.

## Methodology

- **Reference data (cached, unused until now):** `netlogo-models/insalmo_exampleA_results.csv`
  — 3,658 sub-daily ticks, single BehaviorSpace run, seed=98.
- **Python run:** `tests/test_behavioral_validation.py::_run_example_a()`
  pattern, instrumented per-day to track juvenile / adult counts +
  mean juvenile length + outmigrants, since the built-in census only
  emits twice yearly.
- **Metric choice:** five scalar statistics that are stable under the
  PCG64 ↔ Mersenne Twister RNG divergence. Per-individual trajectory
  matching is impossible with different RNGs; distributional summaries
  are the only meaningful comparison.

## Metrics & tolerances

| # | Metric | NetLogo target | Tolerance | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | CH-S juvenile abundance peak | 2,151 on 2013-01-07 | rtol 0.30 | Peak smooths RNG noise, 30% tolerance ≈ 1-sigma at this N |
| 2 | CH-S adult abundance peak | 21 on 2012-07-09 | atol 8 | Small-N metric, absolute tolerance appropriate |
| 3 | CH-S small outmigrant cumulative | 41,146 | rtol 0.20 | Sum-based, most RNG-robust |
| 4 | CH-S juvenile mean length @ 2012-09-30 | 6.23 cm | rtol 0.10 | Intensive quantity (mean), tighter tolerance |
| 5 | CH-S outmigrant median date (50% CDF) | 2013-01-05 | ±14 days | Phenological, 2-week window covers drift from RNG |

Tolerances are deliberately loose — only one NetLogo seed is cached.
Tighten once a multi-seed BehaviorSpace ensemble is available.

## Results (2026-04-19 run, 24.4 min wall)

**Headline: 2/5 pass, 3/5 fail — real calibration drift exposed, not
test-infrastructure noise.** The failures are all consistent with a
single root cause (slower Python juvenile growth → fewer reach the
outmigration length threshold → 21× fewer outmigrants), giving a
clear direction for the follow-up work.

| # | Metric | NetLogo | Python | Delta | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Juvenile peak | 2,151 on 2013-01-07 | within rtol 0.30 | – | ✅ PASS |
| 2 | Adult peak | 21 on 2012-07-09 | 30 on 2012-08-16 | +9 (atol=8) | ❌ FAIL (close — off by 1) |
| 3 | Small outmigrants | 41,146 | **1,943** | **−95%** | ❌ FAIL (large) |
| 4 | Juv length @ 2012-09-30 | 6.23 cm | **4.25 cm** | **−32%** | ❌ FAIL |
| 5 | Outmigrant median | 2013-01-05 | within ±14 days | – | ✅ PASS |

### What the failures tell us

The two large drift metrics (#3 outmigrants, #4 juvenile length) are
not independent. The causal chain is:

```
Python juvenile growth rate is ~32% slower than NetLogo
  → by 2012-09-30 juveniles are 4.25 cm vs NetLogo 6.23 cm
  → fewer fish reach the 8 cm outmigration threshold in year 1
  → 1,943 outmigrants vs 41,146 (21× fewer)
```

Metric #5 (outmigrant median *date*) still passed: the fraction of
fish that do outmigrate still do so on roughly the same calendar
schedule — only the *count* is off, not the timing.

Metric #1 (juvenile peak abundance) passed at rtol 0.30 because peak
is driven by spawn output + early survival, not growth. Growth affects
*length*, not *count*, in the first year. This is consistent with a
growth-only regression.

### Root-cause hypotheses (prioritized by likelihood)

1. **Migration-decision architecture drift** (promoted to #1 after
   reading NetLogo source 2026-04-19). The threshold mismatch is only
   part of the story:

   - **NetLogo InSALMO 7.3** (`InSALMO7.3_...nlogo:2676-2693`): at every
     tick each fish compares *four fitness values* — `drift-fitness`,
     `search-fitness`, `hiding-fitness`, `mig-fitness` — and picks the
     highest. `mig-fitness` represents the *expected survival × growth*
     of migrating out. If local feeding conditions are bad enough,
     even a 3.6 cm fish chooses migration (confirmed in
     `FishEventsOut-r1_*.csv`: hundreds of outmigration events at
     lengths 3.6-4.0 cm on 2012-01-28/29 alone).

   - **Python v0.30.2** (`modules/migration.py:18-27`): `migration_fitness`
     is a pure logistic of *length alone* — `evaluate_logistic(length,
     L1, L9)`. For example_a this is `evaluate_logistic(length, 4, 10)`,
     which gives 0.1 at 4 cm, 0.5 at 7 cm, 0.9 at 10 cm. A 4 cm fish
     with habitat fitness > 0.1 **cannot** choose migration via the
     main path because the logistic floor is 0.1. The supplementary
     length-gated probabilistic path (line 617-634) can't fire either
     because `outmigration_min_length = 8.0` by default.

   This is an architectural rather than parametric drift. No single
   parameter change closes the 95% outmigrant deficit. A proper fix
   requires reshaping `migration_fitness` to include expected
   survival × growth comparison, not just length.

2. **Outmigration threshold semantic** — original #1, now subsumed by
   #1 above. Python's `outmigration_min_length = 8.0` blocks the
   supplementary fallback path, but even lowering it to 4.0 wouldn't
   enable the main fitness path for small fish.

3. **FRY→PARR promotion gate is annual, not continuous** (discovered
   2026-04-19 follow-up Phase-2). `model_day_boundary.py:482` only
   runs the promotion on January 1 each year:
   ```
   if self.time_manager.julian_date != 1:
       return
   ```
   A FRY born in October year N stays FRY until January 1 year N+1
   when age increments to 1 and promotion fires. Meanwhile
   `modules/migration.py::should_migrate` at line 25 blocks anyone
   who isn't PARR:
   ```
   if life_history != LifeStage.PARR:
       return False
   ```
   So Python fish can migrate only in ~50% of the calendar year
   (after Jan 1 promotion), and the initial-population FRY cohort
   from April 1 waits 9 months before any migration is even allowed.
   NetLogo's "anadromous juvenile" status is assigned continuously
   based on size/age, not gated to a calendar date.

4. **`fitness_memory` scale may not match `migration_fitness`** —
   worth verifying. Python's `fitness_memory` at
   `model.py:102-115` = `alpha × growth_signal + (1-alpha) × surv_signal`.
   With default `fitness_growth_weight = 1.0` (example_a doesn't
   override), it's purely `last_growth_rate` — likely in absolute
   units (g/day or similar) rather than 0-1. Meanwhile
   `migration_fitness` is a logistic on [0.1, 0.9]. The comparison
   `mig_fit > best_hab` only makes physical sense when both are on
   the same scale. NetLogo's fitness-for returns
   `(daily_survival × mean_starv_survival)^time_horizon`, which is
   cleanly bounded in [0, 1].
2. **Growth calibration drift** — the 32% length shortfall at the
   2012-09-30 snapshot hints at a lower effective growth rate. Check
   `cmax_temp_table` interp vs NetLogo, `search_prod`, `drift_conc`,
   and the `prey_energy_density` × `max_meal_size` chain in
   `src/instream/backends/numpy_backend/__init__.py` lines 450-500.
3. **`spawn_defense_area` semantic** (calibration-notes.md:632) —
   example_a YAML has 200,000 (cm² in NetLogo; treated as cm radius in
   Python). This would disperse Python redds more than NetLogo, but
   shouldn't affect individual-level growth. Lower priority, but
   worth bundling in a single calibration pass.
4. **`light_turbid_const` offset** (NETLOGO_PARITY_ROADMAP.md:138) —
   small turbidity-interaction constant that shifts feeding
   opportunity. Lower priority, within rtol 0.10 on length alone it'd
   be hard to explain a 32% delta.

### Why these tolerances remain appropriate

The failures are NOT near-misses from tight tolerances. They're large
(32%, 95%, 43%) relative to their allowed drift (10%, 20%, atol 8).
Loosening the tolerances would mask the drift; that's not the goal
of run-level parity. These tests *should* remain red until the
calibration work closes the drift.

## Known spots to check if parity fails

From `docs/NETLOGO_PARITY_ROADMAP.md` + `docs/calibration-notes.md`:

1. **`spawn_defense_area` semantic drift** (calibration-notes.md line 632):
   Python treats the value as Euclidean cm; NetLogo treats as area
   (cm²). example_a sets `spawn_defense_area: 200000` so interpretation
   matters.
2. **`light_turbid_const` offset** (NETLOGO_PARITY_ROADMAP line 138):
   a small per-reach constant that shifts light attenuation.
3. **Outmigration thresholds**: Python uses `outmigration_min_length=8.0`
   default (`io/config.py:321`), NetLogo uses 5/7 cm size classes per
   `parameters-ExampleA.nls:61`. If the outmigrant-total metric fails
   but peak juvenile matches, this is almost certainly the cause.

## Follow-up items (opened by this report)

Each item is worth its own dedicated session/issue. The run-level
parity tests will stay red until they're addressed, which is the
point — red tests tracking real drift, not flaky tests silenced.

- **[HIGH] Reshape `migration_fitness` to include habitat context.**
  The 2026-04-19 Phase-2 investigation (see "Migration-decision
  architecture drift" above) upgraded this from a parameter mismatch
  to an architectural one. NetLogo's `mig-fitness` vs
  `drift-fitness / search-fitness / hiding-fitness` comparison per
  tick cannot be reproduced by Python's size-only logistic
  `migration_fitness(length, L1, L9)` at `modules/migration.py:18`.
  Reference outmigration behavior: 3.6-4.0 cm fish migrating in
  `FishEventsOut-r1_*.csv` on 2012-01-28/29. No parameter change fixes
  this — `outmigration_min_length` lowered from 8 to 4 enables the
  supplementary path, but the main path still needs `length > ~6 cm`
  just to reach 0.5 migration fitness.
- **[HIGH] Investigate juvenile growth calibration** — ~32% length
  shortfall by end of year 2 is well outside RNG noise bounds. Use
  `scripts/_probe_*` pattern to dump per-day length trajectories for
  a few fish + compare to NetLogo FishEventsOut log. Likely candidates:
  Cmax temperature curve, drift-feeding regeneration, or the
  `energy_to_weight` conversion.
- **[MEDIUM] Verify `spawn_defense_area` semantic** — example_a YAML
  has 200,000. If interpreted as radius-cm instead of area-cm² the
  defense zone is effectively infinite. Re-run after fix and see if
  the juvenile peak metric moves.
- **[LOW] Expand NetLogo reference ensemble** — current single seed
  (98) gives no sense of stochastic CI. Run BehaviorSpace with 10-20
  seeds and rebuild tolerances as 2σ rather than guesses.

## Open gaps (follow-up work)

- **Single NetLogo seed only.** Re-run BehaviorSpace with 10–20 seeds
  and build distributional CIs; current tolerances are guesses.
- **Example B not cached.** Only example_a has NetLogo census. Adding
  example_b would cover multi-reach coordination.
- **InSALMO 7.4 requires NetLogo 7.x** which is not installed locally
  (only 6.4.0). The cached 7.4 CSV proves 7.3↔7.4 are byte-identical
  for example_a; no drift concern until 7.x is reinstalled.
- **Baltic case study has no NetLogo counterpart** — the new real-data
  reaches (Atmata / Minija / etc.) are Python-only. Parity claims for
  Baltic require either porting NetLogo (out of scope) or accepting
  that parity is function-level + Baltic-specific ecological plausibility.
